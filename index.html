What Claira does

We handle the appeal. You review and approve.

Claira is an AI system purpose-built for prior authorisation appeals. It reads denial letters, identifies the denial code and payer requirements, flags gaps in your clinical documentation, and drafts a payer-specific appeal letter citing the right medical necessity criteria — drawn from a curated rule library covering the top commercial payers. A physician reviews and approves in under three minutes. We submit it, track it, and report the outcome.

Six human checkpoints before anything is sent. The AI drafts — your team decides.

How it works

Six steps. Six human checkpoints. Most of the work happens without you.

1
Denial ingested

Upload the denial letter — PDF, image, or fax. Claira extracts the payer, denial code, CPT, ICD-10, dollar amount, and appeal deadline automatically. Your team reviews every extracted field before anything proceeds.

2
Triaged and prioritised

Claira classifies each denial — missing documentation, medical necessity, coverage, or administrative — and scores the appeal probability. Revenue at risk and days until deadline are visible at every stage. Your team focuses on the denials worth fighting.

3
Documentation gaps flagged

Before any letter is written, Claira compares your uploaded clinical documents against payer requirements and tells you exactly what's present and what's missing. No more guessing whether you have enough to win.

4
Appeal letter drafted

Claira drafts a payer-specific appeal letter using your clinical evidence and a curated rule library covering the top commercial payers. Every claim in the letter is cited back to a source document. Your billing coordinator reviews the full draft before it advances.

5
Physician approves

The treating physician gets a single-screen summary: what was denied, what the letter argues, which evidence was used. Confirm, edit, or reject — in under three minutes, on any device.

6
Submitted and tracked

Letter and supporting documents assembled as one package. Your team submits through your existing channel — fax, portal, whatever works. Claira logs it and tracks to a terminal outcome: overturned, upheld, or partial. Every appeal has a resolution.

Also in the works

Recovering denials is step one. Preventing them is where this is going.

Most denials happen because the initial PA submission was incomplete — wrong documentation, missing clinical evidence, or a requirement the team didn't know about. We're building tools to catch those gaps before submission, so fewer claims get denied in the first place. And every denial Claira processes today makes those tools smarter tomorrow.

PA requirement lookup

Enter a CPT code and patient plan. Claira tells you whether PA is required — drawn from a curated database, not a live payer call. Response in seconds, not minutes on hold.

Documentation checklists

PA required? Claira generates the specific documentation checklist for that procedure and payer. Not "clinical notes" — but "progress note within 90 days documenting failed PT with duration and outcome."

Clinical data extraction

Upload clinical notes — typed or handwritten. Claira extracts ICD-10, CPT, prior treatments, functional status, and maps them to payer form fields. Your team reviews every extracted field before it's used.

These capabilities are on our active roadmap. Design partners shape what we build and when.

Frequently asked

Questions we expect you to ask.

Is Claira an AI product?

Yes. Claira uses modern language models to read denial letters, extract relevant clinical evidence, and draft appeal letters in the medical language each payer requires. Every output is reviewed and approved by a physician before submission — the AI drafts, the human decides. No appeal is sent without a physician's sign-off.

How will you handle patient data?

Patient data protection is a core design principle for Claira. We're in active build phase, and our data handling, security controls, and legal agreements are being developed alongside the product. Design partners get full visibility into how we're building this, and no patient data will be processed in production until the appropriate safeguards and agreements are in place. We'll walk you through all of it before any live workflow begins.

Do you integrate with my EHR?

Not yet. In early access, Claira works alongside your existing systems — you forward denials to us or we pull them from your payer portals. EHR integration (starting with athenahealth and Epic) is on our roadmap for 2026.

Which payers do you support?

We're building a curated payer rule library starting with the five largest commercial payers — BCBS, UnitedHealthcare, Aetna, Cigna, and Humana — with orthopaedics coverage first. During onboarding, we map which payers account for the majority of your denials and prioritise accordingly. The library expands with every practice we onboard.

Will Claira submit appeals without a physician reviewing them?

Never. Every appeal letter is reviewed and approved by the treating physician before it's submitted. Claira is decision support, not decision making. The physician signs off — we just make it fast.

What happens if an appeal is denied again?

Every appeal is tracked to a terminal outcome — overturned, upheld, or partial. Second-level denials are flagged for your team's review. Peer-to-peer reviews and external appeals are on our roadmap. For now, Claira handles first-level appeals end-to-end.

What does Claira cost?

Pricing is still being finalised with our design partners. Current direction: a monthly subscription per physician, or per-appeal pricing — whichever fits your practice better. Design partners get preferential pricing locked in for their first two years.

When will you be available more broadly?

Denial appeals automation is onboarding design partners now. PA requirement lookup and documentation checklists are on our active roadmap — design partners shape the build order. General availability is planned for mid-2026, once we've built the product around real practice workflows.
