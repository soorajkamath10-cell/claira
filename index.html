What Claira does

We handle the appeal. You review and approve.

Claira is an AI system purpose-built for prior authorisation appeals. It reads denial letters, pulls the relevant clinical documentation from your files, and drafts a payer-specific appeal letter citing the right medical necessity criteria. A physician reviews and approves in under three minutes. We submit it, track it, and report the outcome.

Your team stops writing appeal letters. Your practice stops abandoning revenue.

How it works

Five steps. Most of them happen without you.

1
Denial received

You forward the denial to Claira — or we pull it automatically from your payer portal. We log the denial code, the payer, the procedure, and the deadline.

2
Triage

We identify which denials are worth appealing based on the denial reason, the payer's overturn rate, and your practice's historical data. Low-value appeals are flagged for your review. Winnable ones go to step 3.

3
Appeal drafted

Claira pulls clinical documentation relevant to the denial, writes a payer-specific appeal letter citing the right medical necessity criteria, and packages the supporting evidence. Draft ready in minutes, not hours.

4
Physician approves

The draft appeal is sent to the treating physician for review. They read, edit if needed, and approve with one tap. Most approvals take under three minutes.

5
Submitted and tracked

Claira submits the appeal through the correct channel for that payer and tracks it until a decision is made. You get a notification when the appeal is overturned — and the revenue lands in your next reconciliation.

Frequently asked

Questions we expect you to ask.

Is Claira an AI product?

Yes. Claira uses modern language models to read denial letters, extract relevant clinical evidence, and draft appeal letters in the medical language each payer requires. Every output is reviewed and approved by a physician before submission — the AI drafts, the human decides. No appeal is sent without a physician's sign-off.

Is Claira HIPAA-compliant?

Yes. Claira is built to HIPAA standards and we execute a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with every customer before any patient data is processed.

Do you integrate with my EHR?

Not yet. In early access, Claira works alongside your existing systems — you forward denials to us or we pull them from your payer portals. EHR integration (starting with athenahealth and Epic) is on our roadmap for 2026.

Which payers do you support?

We focus on the payers that represent the majority of your practice's PA volume. During onboarding, we map your top 5 payers and build payer-specific appeal templates for each. Coverage expands with every practice we onboard.

Will Claira submit appeals without a physician reviewing them?

Never. Every appeal letter is reviewed and approved by the treating physician before it's submitted. Claira is decision support, not decision making. The physician signs off — we just make it fast.

What happens if an appeal is denied again?

We track every second-level denial and flag it for your team's review. Second-level appeals (peer-to-peer reviews, external appeals) are on our Phase 2 roadmap. For now, Claira handles first-level appeals end-to-end.

What does Claira cost?

Pricing is still being finalised with our design partners. Current direction: a monthly subscription per physician, or per-appeal pricing — whichever fits your practice better. Design partners get preferential pricing locked in for their first two years.

When will you be available more broadly?

We're onboarding a small group of design partners through early 2026. General availability is planned for mid-2026, once we've built the product around real practice workflows.
